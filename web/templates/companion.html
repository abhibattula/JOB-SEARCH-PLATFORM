Connect Job Engine to your browser

Apply Assist can fill applications right in your own Chrome or Edge — where you're already signed in to job sites — instead of a separate window. This is a one-time, $0 setup.

Once connected, the companion also adds a Discovery badge: browse any job posting (LinkedIn, Indeed, Greenhouse, company career pages) and a small card shows your match score and the company's H-1B sponsorship flag, with one click to Save to Job Engine. It only reads the page and shows its own card — it never clicks or types anything, and scoring happens on your machine.

Checking connection…
  1. Open your browser's extensions page: paste chrome://extensions (Chrome) or edge://extensions (Edge) into the address bar.
  2. Turn on Developer mode (top-right toggle).
  3. Click Load unpacked and choose this exact folder:
    {{ ext_path }}
  4. That's it — the status above turns green within about 30 seconds. You only do this once; app updates keep the companion's files current automatically.
Status still grey after a minute?
  1. Make sure the app is running (it is — you're reading this page).
  2. Click the Job Engine Companion icon in your browser's toolbar. That wakes the companion immediately.
  3. After updating the app, open chrome://extensions and click the ↻ reload icon on the Job Engine Companion card (or restart your browser). Browsers cache an unpacked extension's background script until you do, so a newly updated companion keeps running the old code otherwise.
  4. Confirm the folder you loaded is exactly the path above — if you moved it, click Remove on the extension card and load it again.
Windows shows "unknown publisher" — is that a problem?

No. The app isn't code-signed yet (that costs money and the app is free), so Windows SmartScreen shows a one-time "unrecognized app" notice on the installer — click More info → Run anyway. The browser companion itself loads locally and never leaves your machine.

Prefer not to install anything? Apply Assist still works without the companion — it opens a separate assistant browser window and fills there, exactly as before.
